# Major US Banks Overview

## Summary

- The US banking sector is led by the **Big Four**: JPMorgan Chase (~$4.1T assets), Bank of America (~$2.65T), Citigroup (~$1.84T), and Wells Fargo (~$1.77T)
- Combined Big Four assets exceed $10 trillion — more than double the next 15 banks combined
- The top 50 US banks hold ~$25.4 trillion in aggregate assets (Q3 2025)

## Notes

### The Big Four

#### 1. JPMorgan Chase (JPM)
- **Total assets**: ~$4.14 trillion (largest US bank by far)
- **CEO**: Jamie Dimon
- **Strengths**: Dominant in investment banking, consumer banking, asset management, and trading
- **Strategy**: Aggressively investing in AI and technology; planning ~$10B in additional expenses for credit cards, branch expansion, and AI
- **Global reach**: Operations in 100+ countries

#### 2. Bank of America (BAC)
- **Total assets**: ~$2.65 trillion
- **CEO**: Brian Moynihan
- **Strengths**: Largest US consumer deposit franchise; strong wealth management (Merrill Lynch)
- **Strategy**: Targeting 16-18% ROTCE; heavy investment in digital banking
- **Notable**: Leading mobile banking app adoption

#### 3. Citigroup (C)
- **Total assets**: ~$1.84 trillion
- **CEO**: Jane Fraser
- **Strengths**: Most globally diversified US bank; strong in institutional clients and cross-border transactions
- **Strategy**: Multi-year turnaround/simplification underway; exiting consumer banking in several international markets
- **Recent**: Stock surpassed book value for first time in 7 years; best NII growth among Big Four

#### 4. Wells Fargo (WFC)
- **Total assets**: ~$1.77 trillion
- **CEO**: Charlie Scharf
- **Strengths**: Largest US mortgage lender historically; strong middle-market banking
- **Strategy**: Targeting 17-18% ROTCE; rebuilding reputation post-scandals
- **Recent**: Fed removed asset cap in June 2025 (in place since 2018 fake accounts scandal); stock at record highs

### Industry Trends (2025-2026)

- **Interest rates**: Banks benefiting from higher-for-longer rate environment; NIM expansion
- **AI adoption**: All major banks investing heavily in generative AI for operations, risk, and customer service
- **Digital banking**: Mobile-first strategies accelerating; branch counts declining
- **M&A**: Consolidation continuing, especially among mid-tier and regional banks
- **Regulation**: Basel III endgame rules being finalized; capital requirements in flux
